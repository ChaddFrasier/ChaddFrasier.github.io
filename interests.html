Research

Working at the USGS has sparked my interests to go back to school and dive deeper into the computer science ocean of innovation. I worked on a research project with Dr. Laszlo Kestay where we predicted the Surface Scattering variable of lunar mission data based on camera and lighting metadata. We could predict scattering values with up to 90% accuracy with relatively small training sets from Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera. The goal is to eventually be able to apply specific photometric corrections without using mathematical calculations and instead using a predictive AI model.
